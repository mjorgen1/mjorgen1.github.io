MPhil/DPhil Student in Computer Science · King's College London · mackenzie.jorgensen@kcl.ac.uk

I am a Computer Science PhD Student funded by the UKRI Centre for Doctoral Training in Safe and Trusted Artificial Intelligence at King’s College London. My research focus is on preventing Artificial Intelligence (AI) discrimination, using a combination of sub-symbolic and symbolic AI techniques.

Background: I graduated Phi Beta Kappa and Magna Cum Laude from Villanova University with a Bachelor of Science in May 2020. I studied Computer Science and Philosophy as a Presidential Scholar. As an undergraduate, I completed research projects in the U.S. and Germany in the fields of big data analytics, multi-agent communication and coordination, and hate speech moderation through machine learning. Since I was 14 years old, outreach has been a priority of mine. I founded a non-profit, Women Advancing Tomorrow's Technologists (WATT), which runs coding clubs for young girls in the Seattle area, and I have started similar coding club programs in Philadelphia and Sunderland, UK. I am eager to continue my outreach pursuits in London during my PhD.

Please reach out if you have any queries regarding my academic career thus far, my research, or my outreach pursuits.
